The Economics of California Higher Education

A closer look into the student journey into the California University financial landscape

Visualization Summary

The focus of this visualization is to guide the user through the financial considerations when evaluating a college in California. In general, a large consideration for most prospective students is the earning potential after graduating college. In today's society, it is a large investment by the student to embark on a journey through college. For this reason, I chose to use an interactive slide show to illustrate the financial considerations that potential students must consider when evaluating a college. It does a comparision of median incomes against other common financial considerations such as the % of students using loans, average net spend per year, and the mean debt of students after graduating college.

Each slide in the presentation represents a different financial aspect of the cost of college, as it relates to the earned income of graduating students; to allow the user to focus on that particular financial aspect of cost of college. I've chosen to allow the user to have the freedom to "bounce" between scenes, as to allow the user to easily compare schools for different factors, be enabling links at the top of the page to navigate freely across different visualizations. In order for the user to keep track of the page that they were on, I used active indicator, by highlight the active page in white. No cost aspect is more weighted then the other, thus I allowed the user to freely navigate, instead of following a purely linear narrative. The objective of is to allow user to evaluate schools and draw their own conclusions.

The scatter plot visualization was the best visualization suited for this message, as it clearly shows the relationships between median earned income and the other cost factors when attending different universities. It allows the users to easily identify the colleges that have high earning potential, and would incur the smallest amount of potential costs, by showing where colleges clustered in the visualization in regards to these factors. I enabled tooltips to allow users the ability to get more detailed information about the individual school, and inform the user about this school. The major intent of this visualization was geared specifically to prospective students who are considering the California University system for their studies, and seeing which school met their financial objectives.

Scene: Average Net Costs

The focus of this scene was to take the Average Net Costs, and compare this information against the median earned income of students post-college. The average net costs represents the calculated data based on calculations by the each university cost calculator, which took into consideration annual college costs such as tuition, room & board, books, transportation, food, and subtracted any potential scholarships and grants to arrived at the annual net costs of attendance. Immediately, I wanted to draw the users attention to the cluster of public schools with an annotation. Specifically to show that the range average net costs were relatively small, and that by comparison, the cluster of public schools compared similar to a good majority of private schools in regards to median income. The annotation clears, as the users filters and rescales the visualization using the median income slider.

Scene: Federal Loan

This scene was meant to paint a picture of a students that leveraged a federal loan to finance their college education. Though the percentage of students using a federal loan doesn't quite convey the gravity of financial burden, it does imply the indication of financial assistance needed to fund an education at the school. Hence, in considering this along with the median earned income, it shows the likelihood of paying off a federal loan. I used a line in addition to brief annnoations for the user to see the negative relationship between % of federal loans versus median earned income. This was the most striking insight from looking at this data, showing that schools with students using higher percentages of federal loans, tended to not have a lower median incomes amongst reporting students.

Scene: College Debt

This scene was meant to capture the mean debt that students accumulated post graduation. In concert with the other scenes, it allows the user to answer the question of which college by the numbers represents the best value for long term earning potential. In this scene specifically, private and public schools didn't seem to have as big of spread and distribution in regards to mean debt after college, which varied with the other financial facts such as average net costs and federal loan. Again, I used a circular annotation to point out this observation in the visualization.

Parameters, Triggers, and Events

The parameter used across all scenes is the median income across the California University System. I leveraged a sliding bar to filter based on median income. This sliding bar acted as a filter to trigger an update on the visualization to show the filtered view based on the median income level selection. Upon the event of sliding the bar, visualization would filter and rescale on the canvas, based on relevant school plots. To enable users, to get a more detailed view of the scatter plot, I enabled tips on the hover of individual plotted dots to expose the full detail information of the school, such as the school name, and exact amounts for median income, average net costs, mean debt, and % of federal loan for each scene.

Conclusion

As a summarized conclusion to this essay, a large portion of the public university tend to present high value in terms of lower debt, average net costs, and federal against higher median incomes. In regards to observations, Stanford University represented the best value in terms of the lowest costs considerations against median earned incomes for graduates from that school. This is in part due to the reputation of Stanford and it's ability to attract highly qualified academic candidates. The lower costs are likely due to it's availability and opportunity for students to qualify for a broad spectrum of scholarships and grants.
